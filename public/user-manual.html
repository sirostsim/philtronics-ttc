What is Work Time?

Work Time is a real-time Manufacturing Execution System (MES) built for UK shopfloor operations. When an operator starts working on an assembly job they press START. When they finish they press STOP. The system records exactly how long the job took, who did the work, which workstation it was on, and which works order and route card it belongs to.

Supervisors and managers can see all active jobs in real time on the Wall Board, pause and resume any job, message operators directly, and monitor performance against target times. Quality reporting tracks Right First Time rates and rework hours automatically. All elapsed times shown exclude paused time, so figures always reflect genuine working time.

💡
No technical knowledge needed.

If you can use a smartphone, you can use Work Time. Everything is done with large, clearly labelled buttons.

Signing In

Open Chrome on your device and navigate to the Work Time web address your supervisor gave you.

1
Enter your username

Not case-sensitive.

2
Enter your password

Characters appear as dots.

3
Tap Sign In

Operators go straight to the Timer screen. Supervisors and above go to the Home dashboard. Managers and Administrators also complete a 2FA step if configured.

⚠️
10 failed attempts locks the device for 15 minutes.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Manager and Administrator accounts must use two-factor authentication. Operator and Supervisor accounts use standard password login only.

Setting up 2FA for the first time

A setup screen appears automatically after your first login as a Manager or Administrator.

1
Download an authenticator app

Google Authenticator or Microsoft Authenticator — free from the App Store or Google Play.

2
Scan the QR code shown on screen

Open the authenticator app, tap the + button, and scan.

3
Enter the 6-digit code and tap Enable 2FA

The code changes every 30 seconds. Once confirmed, 2FA is active.

⚠️
Lost your phone?

Contact your administrator — they can reset your 2FA so you can set it up on a new device.

Starting a Job Timer

Operators land directly on the Timer screen after login. Fill in the job details and tap the green START button.

⚠️
One timer at a time.

Stop your current job before starting a new one.

Form Fields Explained

FieldRequired?What to enter
Item NumberRequiredThe part or assembly number. Can be scanned from a Sage 200 job report barcode. Letters, numbers, hyphens and underscores only, max 40 characters.
WorkstationOptionalYour workstation or bench e.g. WS-01. Appears on the Wall Board, history, and exports.
W/O NumberOptionalThe Works Order number e.g. WO-10042. Can be scanned. Required for Assembly Build Times and quality reporting.
Route Card No.OptionalThe individual assembly number within the works order batch, e.g. 3 (meaning the third assembly in the batch). Combined with the W/O Number, this uniquely identifies a single assembly. Required for multi-operator and rework tracking.
Time CheckOptionalMarks this as a formally measured timing. Shows as a blue Time Check badge in all history views.
✨
Route Card No. is new.

If a works order is for a batch of 10 boards, each board gets its own route card number (1 through 10), making every individual assembly uniquely identifiable in the data. This enables accurate multi-operator tracking and rework analysis.

The Rework Prompt

When you enter an Item Number, W/O Number, and Route Card No. that matches a previous completed timer, Work Time automatically detects this and asks you to confirm why you are returning to this assembly.

ℹ️
The prompt only appears when all three fields match.

You must enter Item Number, W/O Number, and Route Card No. for the system to detect a returning assembly.

The prompt shows a summary of time already recorded on this assembly and asks:

Continuing the build

Choose this if the assembly is not yet finished and you are picking up where you left off. The new timer is logged as standard Work time and added to the total time for this assembly.

Re-Work Request

Choose this if the assembly was completed but has been returned for correction. The new timer is logged as Rework time. This records a quality failure against the assembly for Right First Time reporting, and the timer tile on the Wall Board will appear in orange to alert supervisors.

⚠️
Logging rework accurately is important.

Rework timers feed directly into the Quality & Right First Time report. Accurate logging helps management understand which items fail most and where corrective action is needed.

Scanning a Barcode or QR Code

Every field with a ⊞ Scan button can be filled by scanning with the device camera. Item Number, W/O Number, and Route Card No. can all be scanned directly from Sage 200 job report barcodes.

1
Tap the Scan button next to the field you want to fill
2
Allow camera access (first time only)

Tap Allow when Chrome asks.

3
Point the camera at the barcode or QR code

The system reads it automatically. Good lighting helps. A Torch button appears in low light.

4
The field fills automatically

Check the value then continue.

Stopping a Job Timer

Once started the screen shows a large running clock. Tap STOP as soon as you finish the job. The duration recorded is net working time only — any paused time is automatically excluded.

ℹ️
Timer restores automatically.

If you close the browser or switch devices, your active timer restores when you log back in. The elapsed time is calculated from the server so it is always accurate.

Target Time on the Timer Screen

If a manager has set a target time for the item you are working on, a progress bar appears beneath the running clock.

  • Green bar — under 80% of target time elapsed
  • Amber bar — between 80% and 100% (approaching)
  • Red bar — target exceeded

Pausing and Resuming Your Own Timer

Tap the ⏸ Pause button next to the STOP button. The clock turns blue and freezes. Tap ▶ Resume to continue. All paused time is excluded from the final duration.

Working Overtime

Work Time automatically pauses all timers at the end of the working day (16:30 Mon–Thu, 13:00 Fri). If you need to continue working outside these hours, you can override the auto-pause.

✨
Overtime override is new.

When your timer is auto-paused at end of day, an amber ⏱ Override — Working Overtime button appears below the Resume button. Tap it to resume your timer and mark it so the schedule will not auto-pause it again that evening. The override is automatically cleared at the start of the next working day.

💡
Overtime time still counts.

All overtime time is included in the final duration when you stop. The system records exactly how long the job took regardless of when in the day it was worked.

Cancelling a Timer

Within 60 seconds of starting

Tap the Cancel button next to STOP. A confirmation box appears — confirm or tap Keep Running to go back.

After 60 seconds

Only a Supervisor or above can cancel. Ask your supervisor — they must enter a reason which is permanently recorded.

🔒
All cancellations are permanently recorded.

Viewing Your History

Tap the menu then History to see your completed jobs. The default view shows the last 7 days. Rework timers are shown with an amber 🔄 Rework badge so they are clearly distinguishable from standard work.

Supervisor: Home Dashboard

Supervisors, Managers, and Administrators land on the Home dashboard after login. It gives an at-a-glance view of the entire shopfloor, with active jobs sorted by priority — hand raised first, then rework, then overdue, then warning, then on track, then paused.

Wall Board

Tap the menu then Wall Board for the full interactive wallboard. Each tile shows the item number, operator name, elapsed time (counting up live), workstation, W/O number, Route Card No., and a target progress bar if a target is set.

Tile border colours:

  • Green — running normally, under 80% of target
  • Amber — between 80–100% of target
  • Red — target exceeded
  • Orange — this is a rework job (see below)
  • Blue pulsing — timer is paused

The board refreshes every 5 minutes. Tap Refresh for an instant update.

Wall Board Tile Sort Order

Tiles are automatically sorted so the most urgent situations always appear first, top-left. The order is:

PriorityConditionWithin group sorted by
1✋ Hand raisedAlways top
2🔄 Active reworkLongest running first
3OverdueLongest overdue first
4Warning (80%+ of target)Closest to overdue first
5Active, on trackLongest running first
6Paused reworkLongest paused first
7Paused standardLongest paused first

The sort updates on every refresh, so tiles automatically climb toward the top as jobs progress or situations change.

Rework Tiles on the Wall Board

Rework jobs are displayed with a distinctive warm orange background and a 🔄 RE-WORK badge, making them unmissable at a glance from across the shopfloor.

The orange tile immediately signals to a supervisor that this assembly has been returned for correction and may need attention. Rework tiles are sorted above all other paused tiles even if the rework timer is paused.

Wall Board Compact

Tap the menu then Wall Board Compact for the TV display mode. Designed for a large shopfloor monitor — shows as many tiles as possible with operator name, item number, and elapsed time. The same colour logic applies including orange for rework and blue pulsing for paused jobs.

Presence Indicator

Every operator tile on both wallboards shows a small dot next to the operator's name.

  • ● Green dot — operator has an active browser session
  • ● Red dot — operator has closed their browser or logged out

Pausing Timers from the Wall Board

Supervisors, Managers, and Administrators can pause or resume any operator's timer directly from the Wall Board using the ⏸ Pause and ▶ Resume buttons at the bottom of each tile.

ℹ️
Pause buttons are only on the main Wall Board.

The Compact Wall Board is a display-only view.

Messaging Operators

Supervisors and above can send a real-time message to any operator who is currently logged in. Messages appear instantly as a popup on the operator's screen.

1
Tap ✉ Message on the operator's wallboard tile
2
Type your message and tap Send

Up to 500 characters.

3
The message appears instantly on the operator's screen
ℹ️
Check the presence dot first.

If the dot is red the operator is not connected and will not receive the message.

Supervisor: Viewing All History

The History screen has additional filters for Supervisors — search by Operator, Item Number, and Status. Rework timers are shown with an amber 🔄 Rework badge. Use the Status dropdown and select Active (stuck timers) to find any timers that are still showing as active regardless of when they were started.

Manager: Dashboard

Tap the menu then Dashboard for production statistics across all operators. Use the date range and other filters then tap Search. The performance table shows average actual time, min/max, target, and average delta per item number.

Manager: Assembly Build Times Report

✨
Assembly Build Times is new in v2.0.

The Assembly Build Times report in the Reports section groups timer records by Item Number, W/O Number, and Route Card No. It shows how long each individual assembly took, accounting for multiple operators and multiple stints.

Key metrics per assembly card

  • Combined time — total operator-hours across all contributors. Use this for job costing.
  • Elapsed time — wall-clock time from first start to last stop. Use this for schedule planning.
  • Overlap — how long multiple operators worked simultaneously on the same assembly.
  • Contributors — how many operators worked on this assembly.

Each card also shows an operator breakdown table with individual time contributions and a percentage bar. Multi-operator assemblies are highlighted with a purple Multi-operator badge.

Use the filter bar at the top of the section to search by item, W/O, or route card number. Use the Multi-operator only toggle to show only assemblies with more than one contributor.

💡
Export to CSV.

Tap the Export CSV button at the top of the section to download a full export of all assembly records in the selected date range, with one row per operator stint.

Manager: Quality & Right First Time Report

✨
Quality reporting is new in v2.0.

The Quality & Right First Time (RFT) section in the Reports page provides quality metrics based on timer categories. An assembly fails RFT if any timer on it is logged as a Re-Work Request.

Summary statistics

MetricWhat it shows
Right First Time %Assemblies with no rework timers as a percentage of all tracked assemblies. Target: 95%+.
Assemblies trackedTotal distinct assemblies (by W/O + Route Card) in the date range.
Passed first timeAssemblies with no rework timers.
Required reworkAssemblies where at least one rework timer was recorded.
Total work timeSum of all standard Work timer durations.
Total rework timeSum of all Rework timer durations.
Rework as % of hoursRework hours as a proportion of total production hours. Useful for capacity planning.

RFT gauge

A colour-coded bar shows the RFT rate at a glance: green (95%+), amber (80–95%), red (below 80%).

Rework breakdown tables

Two tables show rework frequency and hours broken down by item number (which items fail most often) and by operator (which operators are performing rework). These help identify systemic quality issues and training needs.

1
Tap + Add Target Time
2
Enter or scan the Item Number
3

Manager: CSV Export

On the Dashboard, set your filters and tap Export CSV. The file downloads to your device and opens in Excel or Google Sheets. The export includes Item Number, Operator, Workstation, W/O Number, Route Card No., Start and End times, Duration (net working time), Timer Category (work or rework), Time Check flag, and Status.

Administrator: Managing Users

Adding a User and Resetting a Password

Adding: Tap Admin then + New User. Fill in Username, Full Name, Password (8+ characters) and Role. Tap Create User.

Resetting a password: Find the user in the list, tap Reset PW, enter a new password, tap Reset Password.

Disabling an account: Tap Edit next to the user, untick Account Active, tap Save Changes. History records are kept.

Resetting a User's 2FA

Find the user in the list and tap Reset 2FA. They will be prompted to set up 2FA again on their next login.

🔒
Only reset 2FA when you are certain of the user's identity.

Deleting a Timer Record

Go to History, find the record, and tap the delete button. A confirmation box appears before anything is deleted. Deletion is permanent and cannot be undone.

💡
Cancel All Stuck Timers.

In the Admin page there is an emergency tool to cancel all currently active timers at once. A reason is recorded for every cancellation.

Administrator: Bulk User Upload

✨
Bulk user upload is new in v2.0.

Administrators can create multiple user accounts at once by uploading a CSV file. This is particularly useful when onboarding a new department or a large number of operators.

CSV format

The CSV must have a header row with the following columns:

username, full_name, role, department, password

ColumnValid values
usernameLetters, numbers, dots, hyphens, underscores. 2–40 characters. Must be unique.
full_nameAny text. This is the name shown on the Wall Board and reports.
roleoperator, supervisor, manager (administrators cannot create administrator accounts via bulk upload)
departmentMust exactly match one of: Production, Stores, Test and Inspection, PCB
passwordMinimum 8 characters. This is a temporary password — users should change it on first login.

How to upload

1
Tap Admin then ⬆ Bulk Upload CSV
2
Download the CSV template

Tap the Download CSV Template button to get a pre-formatted file with example rows.

3
Fill in your user data and save the file
4
Upload the file and tap Validate CSV

The system checks every row for errors before creating any accounts. A preview table shows which rows are valid (green tick) and which have errors (red list).

5
Review the preview and tap Create Users

Only valid rows are created. Invalid rows are skipped and reported. You can fix the errors and re-upload.

ℹ️
Maximum 200 rows per upload.

For larger batches, split into multiple files.

Working Hours Auto-Pause

Work Time automatically pauses all active timers outside working hours and resumes them when the working day begins.

ConditionBehaviour
16:30 Mon–ThuAll active timers auto-paused
13:00 FridayAll active timers auto-paused
07:45 next working dayAll schedule-paused timers auto-resumed
Saturday and SundayAll active timers remain paused all weekend
Manually paused timersNOT auto-resumed — operator or supervisor must resume manually
Overtime override activeTimer NOT auto-paused that evening — override clears at 07:45 next working day

All elapsed times always reflect net working time only. A job started Friday at 15:00 and stopped Monday at 09:00 records approximately 1.5 hours — the actual time worked, not the 66 hours the clock ran.

💡
Working overtime?

When your timer is auto-paused at end of day, an amber Override — Working Overtime button appears. Tap it to keep your timer running through the evening. See the Working Overtime section above.

Troubleshooting

I cannot sign in

Check your username and password. Passwords are case-sensitive. After 10 failed attempts wait 15 minutes. If your account may be disabled, contact your administrator.

I am not being asked for my 2FA code

2FA is required for Manager and Administrator accounts only. If you have not yet set up 2FA you will see a setup prompt after login.

The Scan button is not working

Make sure you are using Google Chrome version 83 or later. If camera permission was denied, tap the padlock icon in the address bar, change Camera to Allow, then refresh.

The rework prompt did not appear

The rework prompt only appears when all three fields match a previous timer: Item Number, W/O Number, and Route Card No. Check that all three are filled in and exactly match the previous entry. The prompt looks back 7 days.

My active timer is not showing after a refresh

Tap the menu then Timer. The system restores your active timer automatically. If you see an error, sign out and back in.

My timer was auto-paused and I do not know why

Timers are automatically paused at end of the working day (16:30 Mon–Thu, 13:00 Fri). If you are working overtime, tap the amber Override button to resume without being auto-paused again. If you are within working hours and your timer was paused, a supervisor may have manually paused it from the Wall Board.

I sent a message but the operator did not receive it

Check the presence dot on the operator's wallboard tile. A red dot means they are not currently logged in. Ask them to log in and try again.

The page is not loading

Check the device is connected to Wi-Fi. Refresh the page. Wait 30 seconds and try again if the problem continues.
